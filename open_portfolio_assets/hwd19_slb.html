In Howard the Duck #19, released in December 1977, the titular character embarks on a thrilling adventure as he transforms into a human and navigates the streets of New York City. Written by Steve Gerber and illustrated by Gene Colan, this iconic issue offers a vivid portrayal of urban life challenges and the complex relationships Howard forms during his journey.

As the story unfolds, Howard encounters Mad Dog, a drunken derelict, and later, the enigmatic Lisa Pope, who is captivated by Howard's apathy. A surprising intimate moment between the two characters triggers Howard's reversion to his original duck form, while the menacing Dr. Bong learns of Howard's survival and teleports to New York City to confront his nemesis.

Following a dramatic confrontation with Elton, a former coworker of Lisa's, outside her apartment, an oblivious Howard discovers the cause of his transformation while taking a shower. Despite the revelation, Dr. Bong arrives, determined to eliminate Howard once and for all.

Howard the Duck #19 is a testament to the remarkable storytelling abilities of Steve Gerber and the exceptional artistic talents of Gene Colan. The issue's compelling narrative, memorable characters, and captivating twists make it an essential read for any Howard the Duck fan.
